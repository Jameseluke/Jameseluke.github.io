  1. 1. Street Art

Ideation

James Luke
2017-03-07
Design, University, Web


In the first workshop of the semester, we were challenged to identify both what Melbourne was to us, and what we believed the problems facing Melbourne were.

The first task, drawing mind maps relating to the central bubble of “Melbourne”, highlighted the diverse outlooks and experiences that even a group of very similar individuals (All computer science student’s at the University of Melbourne) could have in regards to the city they live in.

Some drilled down on the areas that they were clearly involved in beyond a passing interest, whilst others focussed on the expansive culture of Melbourne as a whole. Elements that were common to all of our mind-maps were food, drinking, and the street culture of Melbourne, with both public transport and nightlife also making repeat appearances.


The next phase of the ideation process was creating a wall of problems we perceived as afflicting the people of Melbourne. These problems ranged from infrastructural ones, such as delayed train services, to societal issues, such as the cultural disconnect between those living within Melbourne, and those from outlying regions.

Clustering the problems together,
// Clear groupings were apparent
// Other than the so called ‘Miss fits’ such as “not enough drones” and “pubs are closing down”
// Transport, Infrastructure, Poverty

// Look at groupings, no one problem set that engaged with us as a team
// Went back to our Mind-maps and attempted to synthesize the problems with what we as a group thought Melbourne means
// Improve life for the homeless, Help international students settle into university, Connect people who share interests with theme

// Sometimes found ourselves identifying solutions not problems, and then working backwards
// Reoccuring theme was the culture found throughtout Melbourne, be it in the music, the food, or the art
// That’s how we decided to focus on the area of street art

Street Art


// Street art takes many forms, including painted works using aerosol cans, cardboard and paper ‘paste ups’ (Christine Dew in her book ‘Uncommissioned Art’)
// Street art can serve as a tourist attraction, as demonstrated by the famous Hosier lane, and gives the cityscape a unique feel and look
// The city of melbourne has embraced this, even generating guides of where to find the best street art within the CBD

// Can also give people who don’t have the resources to launch more traditional art career a shot

// However, obviously there is the negative side of street art
// The so called “tagging”

// Seen as a menance to life quality and a community’s security
// One of the most evident criminal and disturbance forms
// Potential to impact negatively on both business profitability and private investment
// Related to other criminal activities

// Costs the community around $200 million each year, according to the Australian Institute of Criminology
// City of melbourne has recognised the negative effects of graffiti and come up with a management plan
// So clearly this is a problem that affects the lives of many, and removing or even reducing it would improve the quality of life for those living in Melbourne.
// Likewise an emphasis on what might be considered true “Street Art” might reduce the extent of tagging by providing an alternative

// However the line between the two is not always clearly defined, and this will have to be considered in the solution design phase of this project
