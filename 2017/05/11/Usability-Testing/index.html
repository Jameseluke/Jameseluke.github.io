Usability Testing

James Luke
2017-05-11

Usability Testing

This week we underwent usability testing with a number of participants, in order to gain a more thorough understanding of how our website would be used by real users, what we needed to implement, and further changes we could make to our site in its current state.

One of the core themes that ran through all of the tests was a need for clarity throughout the site and feedback after action is taken by the user. Examples of the former are clarifying the difference between submissions and canvasses, making the availability of certain functions clear to users, and focussing the users attention on the pivotal functionality of the site.

In regards to user feedback, this was brought up multiple times in regards to the registration and login functionality. Users were brought to the home page on registration, but it was unclear whether they had been logged in, or even if they had been successful at registration at all.

Areas of Website Assessed



- First reaction of home page
- Website navigation
- Signup and Login process
- Profile page viewing
- Pretending to be a business making a canvas
- Pretending to be an artist making a submission


Observations



Home Page

  • Wasn’t clear you could scroll down
  • Slideshow is nice, but it wasn’t clear that it was a slide show.
  • Suggestion: click on picture sends you to the artists profile
  • Difficult to know what a canvas was because of CANVS as the app name however easy to understand once properly read the description on home page
  • Suggestion: highlight and reselect words on home page.
  • Slideshow indicators not visible enough
  • They liked the overlay being on top of the background image
  • The slideshow buttons were hard to distinguish

About Page

  • Wasn’t clear that you could scroll down
  • Suggestion: Perhaps fix the wording of some of the paragraphs
  • Suggestion: Make is very clear (and prominent in the about page) what a canvas and a submission is.
  • The about page text was not all uniform, some of the text had spacing with the sides of the page while the first text box didn’t
  • After reading the about page was clear on the purpose of the site, but said it was too long to scroll through and read

Registration/Login

  • Registration was good
  • Login was good
  • It wasn’t clear that you could edit your profile (particularly profile pic and bio)
  • Having both a ‘register now’ and a ‘sign-up’ button was confusing because the differences where hard to tell.
  • Upon registration there was no confirmation that an account was created or that a user was logged in
  • A redirection to the homepage without any kind of notification left users confused
  • It was unclear whether a user was immediately signed in or if an account was actually created

Canvases Page

  • Price for commissioned should be clear for the artist
  • It would be nice to filter your canvases based on price, location, size, canvas type? (i.e. either a wall or a building or a bin)
  • Make/View Submission doesn’t make sense
  • Suggestion: Both seemed confused about what was displayed on the ‘canvases’ page, whether it was relevant to them as property owners or not. Perhaps a short paragraph explaining page at top.
  • Difficulties understanding what ‘Free’ and ‘Commissioned’ meant.
  • Suggestion: Have a ‘filter’ sign and a question mark which explains Free and Commissioned when clicked on. Or change wording.
  • On the canvases page user was confused between canvases and submissions, having some sort of minimal text describing the two to immediately tell users what the purpose of each is would distinguish them better

Individual Canvas Page

  • Suggestion: Indicate how big the canvas is (if it’s paid). Some pictures show entire buildings, do they expect the entire building to be covered?
  • Suggestion: Include some sort of a mention about legalities, and that we’re not reliable for any damages that may incur.
  • Separate the description and the address more so it’s more obvious.
  • Clicking on the submissions should send you to user profile.
  • She asked what exactly the pictures on the right were (the submissions), but when I asked her to guess she guessed correctly.
  • Make a Submission button was good in that it was clear that it is a button.
  • Once a canvas was created, it was difficult to understand the canvas page: what is the big white space? Do I have to click on make submission button to fully upload the canvas?
  • Something indicating submissions go in the white space and a “Your canvas was successfully uploaded” message can be added.
  • Couldn’t tell if there were any submissions by looking at the canvases page
  • Suggestion Show each canvas’s submission count
  • On a canvas submission they were happy to see their submission immediately show up in the list of submissions as validation that their submission was completed, a message would still be valuable
  • A lot of white space on an initial canvas creation page made it unclear where future submissions would go on initial posting
  • No immediate way to tell which artists had posted which submissions on a canvas
  • Suggestion: A hover-over for submissions like the canvases page would work so as to not distract from the art
  • Suggestion: If a canvas job had been completed would there be a way to distinguish which of the submissions was chosen by some kind of marker

General

  • As a business, if I wanted to search for artist there is no way to do that
  • Besides the slideshow, but it wasn’t ideal if I wanted to look through a lot of artwork.
  • Suggestion: a page like canvases but of the artists and/or their work.
  • Suggestion: perhaps incorporate the use of hashtags. The business could search for artwork that meet their design criteria (i.e. #colorful #contemporary #animals #politicalstatement #geometric etc.)
  • Difficulties understanding difference between making a submission and creating a canvas
  • Too much text on about site making it difficult to find what they were looking for
  • Unclear that users had profiles
  • Suggestion Was able to determine from a hyperlink on the profile icon eventually, but needs to be more concrete that a user can access a personalised profile page
  • Approved of the minimalistic design and colour scheme, liked that pages didn’t feel too cluttered with options
  • Enjoyed that the process for submitting a canvas or a submission was extremely similar
